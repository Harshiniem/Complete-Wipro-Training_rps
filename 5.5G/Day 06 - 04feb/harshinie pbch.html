PBCH in 5G NR

Frame Structure, Slot Location & Resource Mapping

PBCH Overview

The Physical Broadcast Channel (PBCH) carries the Master Information Block (MIB), which provides essential system information required by the UE during initial cell access.

Key Characteristics

  • Payload: 24-bit MIB
  • Repetition period: 80 ms (8 radio frames)
  • Modulation: QPSK
  • Channel coding: Polar coding
PBCH is the first physical channel decoded by the UE after cell search. Without PBCH, the UE cannot proceed with system information decoding.

PBCH Location in Frame Structure

SS/PBCH Block – Resource Mapping

PBCH is mapped within the SS/PBCH block and spans 3 OFDM symbols over 20 Resource Blocks (240 subcarriers), centered around DC.

Symbol-wise Content

OFDM Symbol Content Bandwidth
0 PSS 127 subcarriers
1 PBCH + DMRS 240 subcarriers (20 RBs)
2 SSS + PBCH + DMRS 240 subcarriers (20 RBs)
3 PBCH + DMRS 240 subcarriers (20 RBs)

PBCH Processing Chain

  • 24-bit MIB + CRC attachment
  • Polar encoding and rate matching
  • Cell-ID based scrambling
  • QPSK modulation (432 symbols)
  • Mapping to 3 OFDM symbols with DMRS
Polar coding is used in 5G NR PBCH due to its superior performance at short block lengths compared to LTE convolutional codes.
