üîÑ RLC ARQ WORKFLOW STAGES

Stage 1: Data Reception & Buffering

The TX Buffer receives SDUs (Service Data Units) from upper protocol layers (PDCP/IP). Data is queued and prepared for segmentation. This stage manages flow control between layers.

Stage 2: Segmentation

Large SDUs are divided into smaller PDUs (Protocol Data Units) based on the transmission window size and channel capacity. Each segment is sized appropriately for wireless transmission.

Stage 3: Header Addition

RLC AM headers are attached to each PDU containing: Sequence Number (SN), segmentation information, polling bit (P), and final bit (F). This metadata enables reliable delivery and correct reassembly.

Stage 4: Retransmission Buffering

PDUs are copied to the retransmission buffer before transmission. These copies are retained until positive acknowledgment (ACK) is received, enabling retransmission if needed.

Stage 5: Transmission & Scheduling

The TX Routing module schedules PDU transmission based on priority, available bandwidth, and window size. It manages the sending window to prevent receiver buffer overflow.

Stage 6: Air Interface Transmission

PDUs are transmitted over the wireless channel (air interface). During transmission, packets may be corrupted, lost, or delayed due to signal interference, fading, or congestion.

Stage 7: Reception & Error Detection

RX ARQ Control receives PDUs and performs error checking (CRC validation). It monitors sequence numbers to detect missing packets, duplicates, or out-of-order delivery.

Stage 8: Status Reporting (ACK/NACK)

For successfully received PDUs, RX sends ACK (Acknowledgment). For corrupted or missing PDUs, it sends NACK (Negative Acknowledgment) or timeout triggers retransmission.

Stage 9: Retransmission (if needed)

Upon receiving NACK or timeout, TX ARQ Control retrieves the PDU from the retransmission buffer and resends it. This continues until ACK is received or maximum retry limit is reached.

Stage 10: Reordering

The reordering module arranges received PDUs by sequence number. This ensures in-sequence delivery even when packets arrive out of order due to retransmissions or channel variations.

Stage 11: Header Removal

RLC headers are stripped from PDUs after validation and reordering. The extracted sequence numbers and control bits have already served their purpose.

Stage 12: Reassembly & Delivery

Segmented PDUs are reassembled into the original SDU. The complete, error-free message is then delivered to upper protocol layers (PDCP/IP) for further processing.
