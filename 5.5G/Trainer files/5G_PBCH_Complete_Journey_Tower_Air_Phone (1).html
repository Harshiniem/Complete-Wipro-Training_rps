Shuffle the Deck ‚Äî Interleaving

3GPP TS 38.212 ¬ß7.1.5
üÉè Analogy: Imagine you wrote your message on a deck of 864 cards. If someone spills coffee on cards 100‚Äì200, you lose a big chunk. But if you shuffle the deck first, those same coffee-damaged cards now contain scattered bits from all over the message ‚Äî the damage is spread out and much easier to recover from!

Block interleaving rearranges the bits by writing row-by-row into a matrix and reading column-by-column. This spreads adjacent bits apart in time, giving diversity against burst errors.

Beams Sweep Across ‚Äî SSB Beam Sweeping

3GPP TS 38.213
üî¶ Analogy: The tower doesn't broadcast in all directions at once. It's like a lighthouse beam that rotates ‚Äî it sends the SSB in one direction (Beam 0), then rotates and sends it in another direction (Beam 1), and so on. In FR1 below 3 GHz, there are 4 beam positions. In mmWave, there can be up to 64 beam positions! Each beam carries the same PBCH content but with a different scrambling stamp (from Step 2) so your phone can tell which beam direction it received.

The SSB is transmitted periodically (default every 20 ms) within a half-frame (5 ms) burst window. Multiple SSB beams are sent consecutively, each covering a different angular direction. The phone picks the strongest beam.
